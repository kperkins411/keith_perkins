Augmented Reality Laser Safety Googles NASA Langley prototype. Laser operators typically wear safety glasses that filter the color of a laser beam so that it is invisible to the operator. But in order to align a beam with its target, operators need to see it. This application enables laser operators to continue to see a laser through safety glasses by means of an Augmented Reality (AR) headset that presents a view of the laser in a different color. The beam is filtered by the safety glasses yet still visible to the operator.
This application is intended to run on Android based AR headsets and handhelds. It was developed for and tested on: Vuzix M300, Vuzix M100 and Google Glass (explorer edition). It is capable of aggregating mutiple bluetooth and wireless remote camera and instrumentation feeds for display.
Patent pending, NASA collaboration, application repository is private.
