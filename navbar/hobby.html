Augmented Reality Laser Safety Googles Prototype NASA Langley prototype. Laser operators typically wear safety glasses that filter the wavelength of a laser so that it is effectively invisible to the operator. But in order to align a beam with a target operators need to see it, which leads to the risky behavior of peaking around the safety glasses. This application enables laser operators to still see a laser through safety glasses by means of an Augmented Reality (AR) headset that presents a view of the laser in a different color that is not filtered by the operators safety glasses. Presto! Beam is filtered by the safety glasses but still visible to the operator.
This application is intended to run on Android based AR headsets and handhelds. It was developed for and tested on: Vuzix M300, Vuzix M100, Google Glass (explorer edition). It is capable of aggregating mutiple remote camera and instrumentation feeds for display.
Patent pending, NASA collaboration, application repository is private.
